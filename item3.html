UPMA WITH SIYAS COOKHOUSE

Upma Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji or rava)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 inch piece of ginger, grated or minced
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers, etc.), finely chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

instruction:

Heat a pan or kadai over medium-low heat. Add the semolina (sooji or rava) to the dry pan. Roast it while continuously stirring for about 5-7 minutes or until it turns light golden brown. Be careful not to burn it. Remove it from the pan and set it aside. In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, urad dal, and chana dal. Stir and roast until the dals turn golden brown Add finely chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté until the onions become translucent.Add the finely chopped mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers, etc.). Sauté for a few minutes until they start to soften.

Reduce the heat to low and add the roasted semolina to the pan. Stir well to combine the semolina with the tempered ingredients. Pour 2 cups of water slowly while stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Add salt to taste and mix well.

Cover the pan with a lid and let the upma cook on low heat for about 5-7 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Once the upma is cooked, remove it from heat. If desired, you can add a tablespoon of lemon juice for a tangy flavor and mix

To serve:

Garnish the upma with fresh coriander leaves (optional). Serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or a side of your choice.
