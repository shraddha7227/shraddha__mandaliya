SABUDANA KHICHADI WITH SIYAS COOKHOUSE

sabudana khichari Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sabudana (tapioca pearls)
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced into small cubes
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts, coarsely crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil (use ghee for a richer flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sendha namak (rock salt) or regular salt (if not fasting)
  • A pinch of black pepper powder (optional, if not fasting)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped, for garnish (optional)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

instruction:

Place the poha in a sieve or colander and rinse it under running water for about 30 seconds or until it softens. Be gentle to avoid breaking the poha. Drain excess water and set it aside for 10-15 minutes to allow it to fluff up.Heat oil in a large pan or skillet over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.Add chopped onions, green bell pepper, green chilies, and curry leaves to the pan. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onions turn translucent.Sprinkle turmeric powder and salt over the sautéed vegetables. Mix well.Gently add the soaked and fluffed poha to the pan. Toss everything together, making sure the poha is well-coated with the spices. Be gentle to avoid mashing the poha.Mix in the roasted peanuts and drizzle fresh lemon juice over the poha. Stir wellFinally, garnish the poha with chopped fresh coriander leaves and, if desired, some grated coconut.

To serve:

Serve the poha hot with a side of chutney, yogurt, or a cup of tea or coffee.
