EGGLESS CAKE WITH SIYAS COOKH

Ingredients:

  • 1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon white or apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the frosting (optional):
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 and 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

instruction:

Wash the rice and urad dal separately, and soak them along with fenugreek seeds in enough water for about 4-6 hours. Drain the water from the soaked rice and dal, and grind them separately to a smooth paste using as little water as possible. Mix the ground rice and dal batter together, add salt, and mix well. Allow the batter to ferment for 8-12 hours or until it has doubled in volume. Grease the idli molds with ghee or oil. Pour the batter into the molds, filling them about 3/4 full. Steam the idlis in a steamer for about 10-15 minutes or until they are cooked through. You can check for doneness by inserting a toothpick into an idli – it should come out clean when done.

Cook the toor dal in a pressure cooker with enough water until it's soft and mushy. Mash it well and set aside. In a large pot, add the chopped vegetables, chopped onion, turmeric powder, sambar powder, and salt. Add enough water to cover the vegetables and cook until the vegetables are tender. Add the tamarind pulp to the cooked vegetables and let it simmer for a few minutes to blend the flavors. Add the mashed dal to the vegetable mixture and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed. In a separate pan, heat the oil for tempering. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add cumin seeds, asafoetida, dried red chilies, curry leaves, and minced garlic (if using). Sauté for a minute until fragrant. Pour the tempering over the prepared sambar and mix well.

To serve:

Serve the steamed idlis with the delicious sambar along with coconut chutney and/or tomato chutney for a traditional South Indian breakfast or meal. Enjoy!
